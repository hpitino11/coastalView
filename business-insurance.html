What Business Insurance Can Help With

Business insurance helps protect your company from common risks. Including liability claims, property losses, and disruptions that can impact day-to-day operations. We’ll help you choose the right coverage based on your industry, location, and budget.

General Liability

Helps with third-party injury, property damage, and related legal costs.

Commercial Property

Helps protect your building, equipment, inventory, and contents.

Professional Liability

Also called E&O, helps with claims tied to services, advice, or errors.

Workers’ Comp

Coverage for employee job-related injuries (when applicable).

Common Business Insurance Questions

Answers to what we hear most often.

A Business Owner's Policy (BOP) bundles general liability and commercial property coverage into a single, cost-effective package. Individual policies are purchased separately and may be necessary for businesses with more complex risks or specific requirements. We'll help you figure out which approach makes the most sense for your operation.

Florida law generally requires workers' comp for businesses in the construction industry with one or more employees, and for non-construction businesses with four or more employees. Sole proprietors can often exempt themselves, but subcontractors or clients may require proof of coverage. We'll help you navigate what's required in your specific situation.

No, general liability covers bodily injury, property damage, and related claims, but it doesn't cover errors in professional services or advice. That's where Professional Liability (also called Errors & Omissions, or E&O) coverage comes in. If your business provides consulting, design, or specialized services, you likely need both.

It depends on your industry, revenue, number of employees, and contractual requirements. Most small businesses start with a BOP for general liability and property coverage. Contracts with larger clients or government entities often require $1M or $2M in liability limits. We'll help you determine the right coverage amount based on your actual risk exposure.
